Modern Comedy Exhibit

This project was done in a Web Development and Design course led by Alex Turnwall, an accomplished freelance designer and web developer. The topic was this: create an interactive and immersive online museum exhibit that teaches people about a topic of your choice. For my topic, I chose modern stand-up comedy. I led people through 5 decades, discussing the major events, clubs, and stand-ups that were around.

The process started through research and intense wireframing. I think I made about 6 versions of the site before I finally settled on the final project, and even then the specific layout and look was further tweaked

I knew that I didn't just want to create a static webpage that simply listed text and a few pictures. I wanted to excite the eye and constantly draw it to different parts of the page. I decided to do this by including different types of content: many headers and type sizes, varied colors, videos, custom widgets and pictures.

I also created modal pop up windows for particular comedians of the decade that included more verbose information about them, quotes, videos and pictures.

To finish, I let people view a grid of some of the most prolific comedians from all of the decades. Again, modal windows were used to present famous bits and quotes from them.

I was really happy with how this site turned out. It stayed very true to my original vision of how an exhibit should look and was effectively engaging for a viewer. Colors popped, content was interesting and I ended up learning a lot in the process.

SoundToy

This was a strange little project which was inspired by sleep, generative music and Brian Eno. I wanted to create a tool that I could use to play gentle, soothing music while I fell asleep but I also wanted to have a say in the specific sounds and pace of the piece

I set out with the idea that playing samples at specific rates and if you varied these rates between samples, you would end up with beautiful, generative ambient music.

So I did just that! Recording a few different sounds, I loaded them up into the soundtoy and played them at different intervals, animating them on a virtual track. The result was soothing to my ears and looked visually interesting

I consider this SoundToy Version 1... It is imperfect and not designed properly. I intend to expand upon this project, adding more sounds, allowing users to play more with the interaction between samples and offer a sleeker interface. But it's a good step.
